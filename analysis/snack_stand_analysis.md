# Snack Stand Financial & Cost Analysis (2024 - 2026)

Based on a thorough review of the complete transaction log (`fullexpenses.csv`) and the digitized receipts (`Receipts/` directory), here is an analysis of the Snack Shack's expenses, cost changes over the last two years, and considerations for product pricing.

## 1. High-Level Financial Overview

We tracked the exact income and expenses allocated to the **"Snack Shack"** accounts for the last two academic years. 

| Academic Year | Snack Shack Income | Snack Shack Expenses | Net Profit | Profit Margin |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2024-2025** | $23,029.20 | $11,258.28 | **$11,770.92** | 51.1% |
| **2025-2026** | $18,725.75 | $7,558.05 | **$11,167.70** | 59.6% |

**Strong Profitability:** As shown in the full chart of accounts, the Snack Shack is a highly profitable fundraising mechanism. The operation brings in over $11k in profit each year with excellent overall margins exceeding 50%. 

## 2. Cost Analysis: Have items gotten more expensive?

By parsing the individual markdown receipts from vendors like BJ's Wholesale, Restaurant Depot, and Amazon, we can see the exact unit costs of our supplies. Prices on several staple items have crept up.

### Specific Item Costs
* **Mild Cheddar Cheese Sauce:** Increased from **$7.54** to **$8.69** per unit during the 2025-2026 season. 
* **Frito Lay Variety Pack (50 CT):** Averaged **$20.99** per box. 
* **Coke / Diet Coke (35 PK):** **$19.99** per case (approx. $0.57 per can).
* **Gatorade (20 oz):** **$18.49** per case.
* **Nathan's Beef Franks (10/1):** **$25.95** bulk pack.
* **Hot Dog Rolls:** **$3.49** per 5-unit pack.
* **Jumbo Pretzels (Frozen):** **$14.11** per box.
* **Airheads Chewy Fruit Candy (90 CT):** **$14.99** per box (approx. $0.16 per candy).

While bulk candy and drinks have remained relatively stable, dairy products (cheese sauce) and meats (beef franks/patties) are highly susceptible to inflation and have shown price volatility.

## 3. Selling Price Considerations

To offset any rising costs and protect your excellent profitability, we should continue to evaluate selling prices. A standard concession stand should aim for at least a **50% - 70% gross margin** on food items.

Here is a theoretical margin breakdown based on your current unit costs:

| Item | Unit Cost | Suggested Minimum Sell Price | Gross Margin at Suggested |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coke / Soda Can** | $0.57 | $2.00 | 71% |
| **Gatorade (20oz)** | $0.77 (est) | $2.50 | 69% |
| **Frito Lay Chips** | $0.42 | $1.50 | 72% |
| **Airheads (Individual)**| $0.16 | $1.00 | 84% |
| **Hot Dog & Roll** | ~$1.20 (est) | $3.00 - $4.00 | 60% - 70% |
| **Jumbo Pretzel** | ~$0.70 (est) | $3.00 | 76% |

### Key Recommendations
1. **Implement Minimum Margins:** If any item's cost rises such that its margin falls below 50%, the selling price *must* be bumped to the next dollar or 50-cent increment.
2. **Bundle Deals:** To move inventory faster and prevent spoilage, consider $5 or $10 meal deals (e.g., Hot Dog, Chips, and a Drink for $6.00).
3. **Maintain Solid Margins:** Since the Snack Shack is already achieving a 50%+ overall profit margin, continue monitoring bulk purchase prices to ensure that profitability doesn't dip if meat or dairy prices increase further.

# Spiritwear Program Financial & Cost Analysis (2024 - 2026)

## 1. High-Level Financial Overview

Just like the Snack Shack, the **Spiritwear** program is operating as a highly successful fundraiser. By reviewing the `fullexpenses.csv` log, we tracked the exact income and expenses allocated to the Spiritwear accounts for the last two academic years.

| Academic Year | Spiritwear Income | Spiritwear Expenses | Net Profit | Profit Margin |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2024-2025** | $15,132.00 | $6,478.35 | **$8,653.65** | 57.2% |
| **2025-2026** | $13,840.97 | $3,754.77 | **$10,086.20** | 72.9% |

**Massive Profitability:** The Spiritwear program is incredibly healthy. In the 2025-2026 season, the program is operating at an exceptional ~73% margin, bringing in $10k+ in pure profit.

## 2. Cost Analysis: Apparel Cost Inflation

By parsing the digitized invoices from Immaculate Designs and Logo Knits, we can see exactly how the base cost of apparel has shifted over the last two years.

### Specific Item Costs
* **T-Shirts:** Base T-shirts (Grey/Beige) were **$14.00** in 24-25, and Paw Print T-Shirts were **$16.00**. In 25-26, Paw Print T-Shirts increased to **$20.00** (a 25% increase).
* **Crewnecks:** The standard Crewnecks (Navy/White/Grey) were **$26.00** in 24-25. In 25-26, basic Crewnecks increased to **$28.00**, while premium Stripe Crewnecks cost **$42.00**.
* **Hoodies:** Standard Hoodies (White/Grey) were **$36.00** in 24-25. In 25-26, the basic Bulldog/Paw Print hoodies actually cost slightly less at **$32.00**, while premium R/Stripe Hoodies cost **$45.00 - $48.00**.
* **Custom Knit Blankets (Logo Knits):** The 63"x63" blankets were approximately **$43.75** each in 24-25. In 25-26, the base unit price rose to **$46.00** ($49.00 including freight).

### Key Takeaways
Overall, the base cost of standard T-shirts, crewnecks, and blankets has inflated by roughly $2.00 to $4.00 per item over the last year. However, this cost increase is entirely offset by the excellent sales volumes and what appear to be optimized purchasing strategies (like shifting to slightly cheaper base hoodies at $32). The Spiritwear program remains a cornerstone of the booster club's fundraising success.
